Sandwiches-R-Us


It'll always be a great time when you whip out these sandwiches at a party. In the early and mid 20th century there were plenty of variations of the "sloppy joe" such as the chopped meat sandwich, spanish hamburgers, hamburg a la creole, and minced beef spanish style. It is theorized that these sandwiches sold in Iowa in the 1930s were popularized by a cook named Joe.


Ingredients

  • 3 pounds ground beef
  • 1 cup chopped yellow onion
  • 1 cup finely chopped celery
  • 1 bottle tomato based chili sauce
  • Buns


Directions

  1. Crumble the ground beef into a pot over medium high heat and cook until it is evenly browned
  2. Drain out the grease and add the onion and celery and cook until the onions are tender
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and then pour in the chili sauce. Simmer until it is thick and serve on the buns
